# Advisory Board

## Version 1.0 | 2026

The Advisory Board of the **Fair Community Initiative (FCI)** provides independent strategic guidance to support the long-term stability, integrity, and credibility of the Fair Community License (FCL) ecosystem.

The Board operates in an advisory capacity. It does not manage daily operations and does not override the governance structure defined in the [FCI Governance](./GOVERNANCE.md) document.

---

## 1. Purpose

The Advisory Board exists to:

- Provide structured feedback on major license revisions.
- Offer independent perspective on philosophical and structural evolution.
- Strengthen institutional credibility and external trust.
- Support alignment with the FCI Charter and guiding principles.

**The Board enhances deliberation. It does not replace Steward authority.**

---

## 2. Scope of Advisory Input

Board members may be consulted on:

### 2.1 Structural Revisions

Major version updates (e.g., v2.0 changes), introduction of new license variants, or significant definitional shifts.

### 2.2 Governance Evolution

Substantial updates to governance processes or institutional direction.

### 2.3 Ethical and Structural Alignment

Ensuring that license evolution remains consistent with the [FCI Charter](./CHARTER.md) and core principles of transparency, predictability, and sustainability.

### 2.4 Strategic Positioning

Advising on how Fair Community licensing models are communicated and understood in broader industry and community contexts.

---

## 3. Authority and Limitations

The Advisory Board:

- Provides **non-binding recommendations**.
- Does not vote on license releases.
- Does not hold veto authority.
- Does not replace the Stewardâ€™s final determination authority as defined in Governance.

Advisory input is documented where appropriate to preserve transparency.

---

## 4. Composition

The Board seeks balanced representation across four expertise pillars:

| Pillar | Expertise Focus |
|:---|:---|
| **Legal** | Intellectual property, digital law, software licensing. |
| **Industry** | Founders, executives, and operators of sustainable software businesses. |
| **Community** | Independent maintainers, ecosystem leaders, and contributors. |
| **Sustainability** | Economists and researchers focused on digital infrastructure and commons governance. |

Membership is based on expertise, integrity, and alignment with FCI principles.

---

## 5. Engagement Model

The Advisory Board is structured to be **low-friction and impact-driven**.

Engagement may include:

- Virtual strategic consultations triggered by **Major RFCs**.
- Asynchronous written feedback on draft revisions.
- Occasional structured advisory statements when appropriate.

**Meeting frequency is not fixed and is determined by institutional need.**

---

## 6. Appointment and Term

Advisory Board members are invited by the Steward.

Terms may be defined formally in future governance updates. Membership may be rotated or expanded as the Initiative grows.

---

The Advisory Board is currently in its **founding phase**. FCI seeks individuals who:

- Demonstrate expertise within one of the defined pillars.
- Value institutional stability over rapid change.
- Support structured, sustainable licensing models.
- Commit to principled and professional engagement.

## 8. Independence

Advisory participation does not grant governance control over the Initiative. Board members serve in an independent capacity and are expected to disclose any material conflicts of interest when advising on structural matters.
